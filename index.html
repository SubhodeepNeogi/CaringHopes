STRESS

How to control stress?

● Stop where you are and take a deep breath.

● Exercise- daily 15 minutes of exercise can reduce stress ( try to enjoy while doing it)

● Detach yourself from the situation/ introspect/ meditate.

● Reduce intake of caffeine and alcohol

ANXIETY

How to control anxiety?

● Exercise- daily 15 minutes of exercise can reduce stress ( try to enjoy while doing it)

● Practicing yoga and meditation.

● Reduce intake of caffeine and alcohol

● Try to get enough sleep.

● Spend time with your friends and family.

INSOMNIA

How to control insomnia?

● Stay calm and take a deep breath.

● Engage yourself in your favourite hobbies.

● Medication is the best way to deal with lack of energy.

● Listen some good music or read some good stuff before go to bed.

● Reduce intake of caffeine and alcohol.

MEMORY PROBLEM

How to sharpen your memory?

● Establish Your Own Routine.

● Create Stories In Your Mind.

● Write Down Things.

● Have a Mental Workout.

● Eat more foods which will improve your memory.

CONCENTRATION PROBLEM

How to improve concentration?

● Try games like Sudoku, Crossword Puzzles, Chess, Jigsaw Puzzles, Word Search or Scramble, Memory Games

● Improve sleep. Maintain proper sleep timing to have a perfect sleep cycle.

● Do exercise regularly and routinely.

EXAM PHOBIA

Things To Do Before Exam:-

● Planning is the key to good revision.

● Take regular breaks. You Deserve It.

● Eat proper meal.

● Get creative. Make your own revision material.

You are NOT Alone

1 in every 8 people in the world live with a mental disorder

In 2019, 1 in every 8 people, or 970 million people around the world were living with a mental disorder, with anxiety and depressive disorders the most common. In 2020, the number of people living with anxiety and depressive disorders rose significantly because of the COVID-19 pandemic. Initial estimates show a 26% and 28% increase respectively for anxiety and major depressive disorders in just one year. While effective prevention and treatment options exist, most people with mental disorders do not have access to effective care. Many people also experience stigma, discrimination and violations of human rights.

70 million people in INDIA suffer from common and severe mental disorders

In his address, Shri Mansukh Mandaviya, the Union Health Minister highlighted the importance of mental health. ❝It is intrinsically linked to physical health and wellness, and firms an integral part of our traditional knowledge❞, he said. The Union Health Minister, Government of India underscored the importance of inclusion of understanding of mental health in the curriculum of teachers, as they form a pillar of support for children. ❝The role of parents, families and communities in listening to children and supporting them to talk about mental health issues helps to address them early❞
